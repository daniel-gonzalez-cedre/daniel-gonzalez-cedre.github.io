About Me
I'm a third year Ph.D. student studying Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame advised by Tim Weninger. My research is primarily focussed on (temporal) graph mining and graph grammars. I am also interested in Bayesian inference and stochastic analysis, particularly on complex networks and graph models.
In my spare time, I like to play music and lift weights.
I am bilingual in English and Spanish, and I am currently learning Mandarin Chinese. I was born in La Habana, Cuba.
