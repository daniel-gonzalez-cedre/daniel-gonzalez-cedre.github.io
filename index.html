About Me
I am a third year Ph.D. student studying Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame advised by Tim Weninger.
My research is primarily motivated by the desire to understand how graphs evolve over time. To that end, I focus on studying and developing interpretable models for dynamic networks.
I am also interested in how users interact with and influence each other on social networks, especially when misinformation and disinformation are involved.

For fun, I like to boulder, play the guitar & cello, and weightlift.
